About Indian Navy

  • The Indian Navy is the naval branch of the Indian Armed Forces.
  • It protects India’s maritime borders and interests.
  • The Navy operates in the Indian Ocean region.
  • It was officially established in 1950 after India became a republic.
  • The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Navy.
  • The Navy ensures safe trade routes and maritime security.
  • It operates aircraft carriers, destroyers, frigates, and submarines.
  • INS Vikramaditya and INS Vikrant are major aircraft carriers.
  • The Navy conducts anti-piracy and surveillance operations.
  • It also participates in international naval exercises.
  • The Indian Navy plays an important role in disaster relief operations.
  • The motto of the Indian Navy is "Sham No Varunah" (May the Lord of Water be auspicious unto us).
  • The Navy symbolizes strength, courage, and maritime power.

Major Operations of Indian Navy

  • Operation Trident (1971)

    Conducted during the Indo-Pak War of 1971. The Indian Navy attacked Karachi harbor causing heavy damage. It was a historic naval victory for India.

  • Operation Python (1971)

    Follow-up strike after Operation Trident. Indian warships again targeted Karachi port. The mission further weakened enemy naval power.

  • Operation Cactus (1988)

    The Navy supported the mission to stop a coup in Maldives. Indian forces restored peace and stability. It showed India’s strong regional presence.

  • Operation Raahat (2015)

    The Navy evacuated citizens from Yemen during conflict. Warships and aircraft were used for rescue. It became a major humanitarian mission.

Warships of Indian Navy

  • INS Vikramaditya

    INS Vikramaditya is a major aircraft carrier of the Indian Navy. It serves as a floating airbase in the Indian Ocean. The carrier can operate fighter jets and helicopters. It plays a vital role in strengthening India’s naval power.

  • Submarines

    Indian Navy submarines operate underwater for strategic missions. They are used for surveillance and defence operations. Some submarines are equipped with advanced missile systems. Submarines provide stealth and strong deterrence capability.

  • Destroyers

    Destroyers are powerful warships equipped with advanced weapons. They are used to protect aircraft carriers and other vessels. Indian destroyers have modern radar and missile systems. They play a key role in maritime security operations.
