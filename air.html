About Indian Air Force

  • The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air arm of the Indian Armed Forces.
  • It was officially established on 8 October 1932.
  • The IAF protects Indian airspace from external threats.
  • It plays a vital role in modern warfare and national security.
  • The Air Force operates advanced fighter jets like Rafale and Sukhoi Su-30.
  • It also uses transport aircraft for rescue and relief missions.
  • The IAF conducts surveillance and reconnaissance operations.
  • It has participated in major operations like Kargil War.
  • The Air Force helps during natural disasters and emergencies.
  • It uses modern radar and missile defence systems.
  • The motto of the Indian Air Force is "Touch the Sky with Glory".
  • The IAF symbolizes courage, strength, and technological excellence.

Important Missions of Indian Air Force

  • Operation Safed Sagar (1999)
    The Indian Air Force played a major role during the Kargil War. Fighter jets were used to target enemy positions in high-altitude areas. It was the first large-scale use of air power in the Kashmir region.


  • Balakot Air Strike (2019)
    The Air Force conducted precision air strikes on terrorist camps. The mission was carried out using Mirage 2000 fighter aircraft. It demonstrated India's strong response against terrorism.


  • Operation Rahat (2013)
    The Indian Air Force rescued thousands during Uttarakhand floods. Transport aircraft and helicopters were used for evacuation. It became one of the largest humanitarian air rescue missions.

Aircraft

Aircraft of Indian Air Force

  • Rafale Fighter Jet

    Rafale is a modern multi-role fighter aircraft used by the Indian Air Force. It is equipped with advanced radar and missile systems. The jet can perform air superiority and ground attack missions. Rafale strengthens India's air defence capabilities.

  • Sukhoi SU-30

    Sukhoi SU-30MKI is a powerful twin-engine fighter aircraft. It is one of the main combat aircraft of the Indian Air Force. The aircraft is known for its speed, range, and maneuverability. It plays a key role in protecting Indian airspace.

  • Tejas

    Tejas is an indigenously developed light combat aircraft. It is designed and manufactured in India under Make in India. The aircraft is lightweight and highly agile. Tejas represents India's growing defence technology strength.
