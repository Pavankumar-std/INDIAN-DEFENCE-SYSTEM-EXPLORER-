    About Indian Army

    • Approximately 12 lakh active personnel.
    • The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces.
    • It protects India’s borders from external threats and enemies.
    • The Indian Army was officially established in 1947 after independence.
    • It is one of the largest standing armies in the world.
    • The Army operates in extreme conditions like deserts, mountains, and glaciers.
    • It played a major role in wars like the Kargil War (1999).
    • Indian soldiers serve with discipline, courage, and dedication.
    • The Army also helps during natural disasters like floods and earthquakes.
    • It uses modern weapons, tanks, missiles, and advanced technology.
    • The motto of the Indian Army is "Service Before Self".

    Weapons

    • Arjun Tank
    • The Arjun Tank is a main battle tank developed by India. It was designed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). The tank is equipped with a powerful 120 mm gun and advanced armor protection. It plays an important role in strengthening India’s land defence capabilities
    • INSAS Rifle
    • The INSAS Rifle is a standard infantry weapon used by the Indian Army. INSAS stands for Indian Small Arms System. It was developed in India to replace older rifles in the 1990s. The rifle is lightweight, reliable, and suitable for different combat conditions.
    • BrahMos Missile
    • The BrahMos Missile is a supersonic cruise missile developed by India. It is a joint venture between India and Russia. The missile can be launched from land, sea, air, and submarines. BrahMos is one of the fastest cruise missiles in the world and strengthens India’s defence powe

    Major Missions of Indian Army

    • Operation Vijay (1999) – Successfully recaptured the Kargil heights during the Kargil War. Indian soldiers fought bravely in extreme high-altitude conditions. This mission remains a symbol of courage and national pride.

    • Operation Meghdoot (1984) – Secured the strategic Siachen Glacier, the highest battlefield in the world. The Army took control of key positions before enemy forces could act. It shows the endurance and strength of Indian soldiers in harsh climates.

    • Surgical Strikes (2016) – Conducted precise cross-border strikes against terrorist camps. The operation was a strong response to terrorism. It demonstrated India’s capability to act swiftly and decisively.

    • Operation Rakshak – Ongoing mission to maintain internal security in Jammu and Kashmir. The Army works to prevent insurgency and protect civilians. It ensures peace and stability in sensitive regions.
